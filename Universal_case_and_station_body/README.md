# Universal case and station body

The design of the universal case is developed as a balanced solution for transporting and deploying an FPV drone ground control station in the field. The main focus during design was placed on combining reliability, mobility, and manufacturing accessibility.

<img width="600" alt="General view of finished cases" src="picture/general_view_of_finished_cases.jpg" />

## Key design features:
<ul>
<li><b>Compactness and mobility:</b> The body dimensions are optimized to ensure maximum operator mobility. The compact form factor of the case allows for convenient transport of the station in a backpack or as a separate small-sized piece of equipment.</li>

<li><b>Optimization for additive manufacturing:</b> The geometry of the parts is designed for 3D printing on most common FDM printers. The maximum dimensions of individual elements do not exceed 250 × 250 × 250 mm, making the project feasible on budget equipment.</li>

<li><b>Cost effectiveness:</b> The design incorporates the principle of minimizing manufacturing costs. Using affordable materials (PETG/ABS type plastics) and standard hardware products significantly lowers the cost of the finished product without losing functionality.</li>

<li><b>Operational durability and repeatability:</b> The design has a sufficient safety margin for intensive use. Technical solutions make it easy to reproduce the body in local or field workshops.</li>

<li><b>Modular architecture and maintainability:</b></li>
<ul>
<li>The body consists of separate interchangeable modules.</li>

<li>High maintainability: in case of mechanical damage, it is sufficient to replace only the specific element.</li>

<li>Hardware unification: Most connections are made using standard metric fasteners (M3 screws and nuts), which simplifies assembly and component logistics.</li>
</ul>
</ul>
